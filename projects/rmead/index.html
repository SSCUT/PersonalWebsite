Rugged Multi-Environment Analysis Device (RMEAD)

A deployable environmental monitoring device designed to collect critical sensor data in remote or hazardous areas, while keeping people at a safe distance.

Role: Mechanical + Systems Design SolidWorks Embedded C++ RF Communication

Problem

Many environments are unsafe or impractical to enter without prior measurement. Existing options (fixed stations or UAVs) can be expensive, infrastructure-dependent, or limited in deployment time.

Solution

RMEAD is a self-contained sensing payload designed for rugged deployment. It collects temperature, humidity, pressure, gas/VOC indicators, and light levels, and transmits readings over RF to a nearby receiver.

My role

  • Created initial concepts and SolidWorks models; iterated CAD for 3D printing constraints.
  • Selected and validated sensors; wired and tested prototypes.
  • Built and tested embedded code + RF data pipeline.
  • Tested parachute deployment and landing system behavior.

Key engineering decisions

  • RF over networked comms: independence from infrastructure and low power at moderate range.
  • Foam damping over springs: reduced rebound and tip-over risk during impact tests.
  • Sensor selection: balanced capability, reliability, and budget constraints.

Outcome

Delivered a functioning prototype and demonstrated sensor readout + communication. The project was later featured in Northeastern Global News.
